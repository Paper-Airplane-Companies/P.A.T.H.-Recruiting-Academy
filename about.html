About P.A.T.H. Recruiting Academy

Built from real recruiting experience across every level of hiring.

P.A.T.H. Recruiting Academy was created by Brandon Labonte, an HR and recruiting leader with experience dating back to 2009. Over the course of his career, Brandon has worked in consulting, high-volume recruiting, operational hiring, and senior HR leadership roles as high as Vice President of Human Resources.

That experience shaped one clear belief: better hiring starts with better conversations. Across industries, job levels, and company environments, candidates responded far more strongly when the conversation centered on their situation, motivations, frustrations, and goals than when it focused too early on the job itself. That pattern became the foundation of P.A.T.H.

Why P.A.T.H. exists

A better way to guide recruiting conversations.

Brandon has recruited for and hired roles at nearly every level of the organization — from executive positions like COO and CFO to skilled trades, field labor, and frontline operational roles. That range exposed a pattern that repeated itself again and again: when candidates were invited to talk about their current reality, what was missing, and what they actually wanted next, the conversation became more honest, more useful, and more productive.

P.A.T.H. was built from that pattern recognition. It was not created as a theory-only training model or another script-heavy recruiting system. It was built from real hiring experience across different levels, different roles, and different organizational pressures.

  • Move beyond surface-level qualification and early job pitching.
  • Help candidates talk through what matters, not just what appears on paper.
  • Create a framework recruiters can repeat, leaders can coach, and teams can scale.
  • Strengthen the connection between conversation quality and hiring performance.

From executive candidates to hourly workers, the principle stayed consistent: when the conversation is about the person first, the outcome is almost always stronger than when the conversation is about the job first.

Core philosophy

The principles behind the framework.

P.A.T.H. is built around the belief that recruiting should feel more consultative and less transactional. Candidates make stronger decisions when they have the space to think, talk, and clarify what they actually want. Recruiters perform better when they have structure, not just pressure to move fast.

Questions over pitching

The recruiter guides the conversation through thoughtful questions instead of relying too early on persuasion, pressure, or a generic role pitch.

Self-discovery over assumption

Better conversations help candidates verbalize frustration, goals, priorities, and fit for themselves.

Structure over instinct

Recruiters need a repeatable process that improves consistency, coaching, and performance across the team.
